Scotiabank Profit Beats Estimates

5:45 PM EST
November 29, 2016
Share on FacebookShare on Twitter
Share on LinkedInShare on RedditShare on Google+E-mailShare on TwitterShare on WhatsApp

Bank of Nova Scotia posted profit that beat analysts’ expectations, fueled by gains in capital markets and domestic banking. Net income for the fiscal quarter ended Oct. 31 climbed 9.1 percent to C$2.01 billion ($1.5 billion), or C$1.57 a share, from C$1.84 billion, or C$1.45, a year earlier, Canada’s third-largest bank said Tuesday in a statement. Profit excluding some items was C$1.58 a share, beating the C$1.51 average estimate of 17 analysts surveyed by Bloomberg. Bloomberg News' Doug Alexander reports. (Source: Bloomberg)
